NDIS Plan Mangement

For some NDIS participants and their families this is where things start to become a bit complicated.

If your NDIS Plan includes Plan Management then you will need to source a provider that your trust, that will not treat you as a number and will have your best interests at heart. There are a lot national and international of plan providers you can choose from. However, if you choose Assure Care Australia, you will become part of our family, no question will be too big or too small and we will make sure you are no emergency will be too inconvenient. We promise to have you and your NDIS plan and finances covered.

To engage Assured Care Australia as your NDIS Plan Management providers follow these easy steps:

  • Step 1 – Give us a call on 1800 800 110 or contact us to schedule an appointment.
  • Step 2 – During your meeting, Assured Care Australia will outline how we will manage your NDIS Plan, remember to ask plenty of questions, we want you to feel comfortable with us as your NDIS Plan Management providers.
  • Step 3 – Assured Care Australia will DRAFT a Service Agreement on your behalf, this is a legal document which will outline each parties’ responsibilities and will describe how and when the support will be provided.

Remember we work for you and we are only a phone call, text message or email away.

NDIS Support Coordination

If you have Support Coordination as part of your NDIS Plan, Assured Care Australia can help manage your supports.

Assured Care Australia will:

  • Manage all aspects of your support services on your behalf, this includes organising appointments,.XXXXXXXX
  • Organise Service Agreements with your chosen service providers. These agreements will confirm the frequency and costs of the services.

You can choose to use existing support services, choose a new service provider or select from the many services provided by Assured Care Australia – Its always your choice, and you can always change providers.

Step 3 - Prepare for your NDIS planning meeting.

Your NDIS plan is all about your goals, your activities, and your independence! So before your planning meeting think about the goals and activities that are important to you. What are the skills you want to learn to help you become more active or independent.

  • Think about what supports you currently receive, if your happy with the providers, and what could really help you achieve your goals. Look at the NDIS website to find out what the NDIS funds and how the NDIS works.
  • Gather all your relevant reports and assessments, your planner will want to see them
  • Have a go at completing the NDIS – Planning Booklet 2. It’s a great resource that could help you itemise and communicate your activities to your planner.
  • Organise a meeting with a NDIS planner or Local Area Coordinator (LAC) at a time that suits you

If you are having trouble, get in contact with the Assured Care Australia team. We are local and Territory owned and operated.

Step 4 - Read and understand your DRAFT NDIS plan.

  • Make sure your read your plan carefully, or get someone to help you.
  • Check your details like your name and address
  • Take a moment to understand what funds and services you have been allocated, and if your happy with your plan then you should notify your NDIS planner or LAC.

What if you are not happy with your DRAFT plan?

  • If you are not happy with your plan, you should contact your NDIS planner or LAC and discuss your concerns.

If you are having trouble please feel free to get in contact with one of our friendly Assured Care Australia team members. We are local and Territory owned and operated.
