Getting Prepared For The NDIS

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) enables people with ability to access services to help them live their best life. However, navigating the NDIS can prove to be a challenge for participants, their families, and carers. That’s why Assured Care Australia has created an easy guide to help you:

  • Step 1 - Find out if you are eligible for the NDIS
  • Step 2 - Apply for the NDIS
  • Step 3 - Prepare for your NDIS planning meeting.
  • Step 4 - Read and understand your DRAFT NDIS plan.

Step 1 - Find out if you are eligible for the NDIS

  • Question 1 - Are you aged between 7 and 65?
  • Question 2 - Do you live in Australia and have Australian residency?
  • Question 3 - Do you usually need support from a person because of a permanent and significant disability?
  • Question 4 - Do you use special equipment because of a permanent and significant disability?
  • Question 5 - Do you need some supports now to reduce your future needs?

If you answered YES to all five questions, then you may be eligible for the NDIS.

Find out how to apply.

For more information go to www.ndis.gov.au/applying-access-ndis/how-apply

If you answered NO to any of these questions then it is unlikly that you are eligable to the NDIS. However, you may be able to access other supports. For other supports click here.

Step 2 - Apply for the NDIS

If you met the criteria and you would like to become a participant of the NDIS, you should apply.

To apply for the NDIS you can do one of the following:

  • call the NDIA on 1800 800 110 and ask to make an Access Request
     
  • complete and submit a Access Request Form, once completed email it to NAT@ndis.gov.au;
  • contact your Local Area Coordinator (LAC) and ask to make an Access Request. Find your LAC here.

For more information go to https://www.ndis.gov.au/applying-access-ndis/how-apply

Step 3 - Prepare for your NDIS planning meeting.

Your NDIS plan is all about your goals, your activities, and your independence! So before your planning meeting think about the goals and activities that are important to you. What are the skills you want to learn to help you become more active or independent.

  • Think about what supports you currently receive, if your happy with the providers, and what could really help you achieve your goals. Look at the NDIS website to find out what the NDIS funds and how the NDIS works.
  • Gather all your relevant reports and assessments, your planner will want to see them
  • Have a go at completing the NDIS – Planning Booklet 2. It’s a great resource that could help you itemise and communicate your activities to your planner.
  • Organise a meeting with a NDIS planner or Local Area Coordinator (LAC) at a time that suits you

If you are having trouble, get in contact with the Assured Care Australia team. We are local and Territory owned and operated.

Step 4 - Read and understand your DRAFT NDIS plan.

  • Make sure your read your plan carefully, or get someone to help you.
  • Check your details like your name and address
  • Take a moment to understand what funds and services you have been allocated, and if your happy with your plan then you should notify your NDIS planner or LAC.

What if you are not happy with your DRAFT plan?

  • If you are not happy with your plan, you should contact your NDIS planner or LAC and discuss your concerns.

If you are having trouble please feel free to get in contact with one of our friendly Assured Care Australia team members. We are local and Territory owned and operated.
